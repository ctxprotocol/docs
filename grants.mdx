<Warning>
**🚫 What We Don't Fund**

We reject proposals that:

- **Wrap a single public API without adding value**: Calling CoinGecko and returning the JSON isn't a product. For Query mode, add analysis, scoring, or synthesis. For Execute mode, normalize across multiple sources into a consistent typed schema — that's the value. If the likely buyer would rather own the integration than pay through Context, it is Tier B at best unless you add strong normalization, provenance, partial-failure handling, caching, rate-limit protection, or multi-source abstraction.
- **Chase dead or saturated markets**: Meme coin tooling, yield aggregators, and rug checkers had their moment. The alpha is gone.
- **Unbundle subscriptions that AI itself is killing**: SEO tools (SEMrush, Ahrefs, Moz), keyword research platforms, and backlink analyzers exist because Google Search exists. As users shift to AI chatbots, these subscriptions are in structural decline. We don't fund cheaper versions of dying products. Ask: "Is the subscription I'm unbundling itself being disrupted by AI?" If yes, don't apply.
- **Compete with dominant free alternatives**: If Semantic Scholar, Etherscan, or Google News already does it for free, your tool needs a 10x differentiated angle.
- **Lack evidence of demand**: "This would be useful" isn't enough. Show us the expensive subscription people are paying for (Query tools) or the fragmented APIs developers are struggling to normalize (Execute tools). The evidence must support the exact feature or primitive you are pitching, not just the broad vertical.

**The bar:** Can you find real Twitter threads, forum posts, or Reddit threads of people complaining or evangelising about paying for this specific answer product — or developers complaining about the exact integration and normalization pain your Execute primitive solves? If not, don't apply.
</Warning>

## 🏆 Funding Tiers

### What Makes a Tier S Tool?

It depends on who you're building for.

**For Query mode** (Context is the librarian — \$0.10/response):

We pay for **answers**, not raw data dumps. Tier S Query tools do the heavy lifting so the AI can synthesize immediately.

| Feature | Raw Dump (❌) | Curated Intelligence (✅) |
|---------|--------------|--------------------------|
| **Crypto** | "Here's the transaction list" | "Top 5 whales accumulating this token right now" |
| **Finance** | "Here's the SEC filing PDF" | "Key risk factors extracted with sentiment score" |
| **E-commerce** | "Here are Amazon search results" | "Estimated monthly revenue: \$47K, trending up 23%" |
| **Competitive Intel** | "Here's their homepage HTML" | "Tech stack: Stripe, Vercel, Segment. 2.3M monthly visitors" |

**For Execute mode** (developer's agent is the librarian — \$0.001/call):

We pay for **normalized, typed, reliable data** that an agent can iterate over. Tier S Execute tools eliminate the pain of integrating fragmented APIs.

| Feature | Single API Wrapper (❌) | Normalized Data Product (✅) |
|---------|------------------------|------------------------------|
| **Crypto** | "Here's Binance prices" | "Cross-exchange prices in unified `{ exchange, pair, price, volume }` schema" |
| **Finance** | "Here's one SEC filing" | "Parsed 10-K/10-Q with structured metrics, comparable across companies" |
| **E-commerce** | "Here's one retailer's data" | "Product pricing across Amazon, Walmart, Target in consistent format" |
| **Competitive Intel** | "Here's one data source" | "Tech stack + traffic + hiring signals normalized per domain" |

## 🔍 How to Find Unbundling Opportunities

Before pitching, do this research. Here's a real example of how to identify a fundable tool:

### Example: Finding a SensorTower Unbundling Opportunity

**Step 1: Search for frustrated paying users**

Go to [Grok](https://grok.com) and search Twitter:
- `"SensorTower" "pay" OR "subscription" OR "expensive"`
- `"app analytics" "not worth" OR "canceled" OR "only need"`

**Step 2: Find the specific pain point**

You might find tweets like:
> *"I pay \$400/month for SensorTower just to check competitor download estimates before investor calls. Everything else in the dashboard I never touch."*

> *"SensorTower is worth it for the App Store keyword rankings alone. Rest is noise."*

**Step 3: Isolate the \$0.10 response**

From this research, you'd identify: **"App download estimates by bundle ID"** is the core value. Build a tool that delivers just that. The specific data point people are paying \$5K/year to access occasionally.

**Step 4: Pitch with evidence**

Your proposal should say: *"SensorTower charges \$5K/year. Users on Twitter report paying primarily for download estimates. Here's my tool that delivers that specific data point for \$0.10/response. Here are 3 tweets proving demand."*

<Info>
**This is the bar for Query-mode tools.** Every idea in the curated intelligence section has been evaluated against this standard.
</Info>

### Example: Finding an Execute-Mode Normalization Opportunity

**Step 1: Search for frustrated developers**

Go to [Grok](https://grok.com) and search Twitter, GitHub issues, or Reddit:
- `"different schema" "exchange" OR "API" site:github.com`
- `"normalize" "crypto" "API" "pain" OR "nightmare" OR "inconsistent"`

**Step 2: Find the integration pain**

You might find posts like:
> *"Spent 3 days just getting Binance, Coinbase, and Kraken order book data into the same format. Every exchange returns depth differently."*

> *"Why does every SEC filing parser return a completely different JSON structure? I just want balance sheet data in a consistent schema."*

**Step 3: Isolate the \$0.001 method**

From this research, you'd identify: **"normalized order book data across exchanges"** is the value. Build a tool where `get_orderbook("BTC/USDT", "binance")` and `get_orderbook("BTC/USDT", "kraken")` return the exact same typed schema. The developer pays fractions of a cent per call instead of building and maintaining the normalization themselves.

**Step 4: Pitch with evidence**

Your proposal should say: *"Developers integrating multiple exchange APIs spend days normalizing schemas. Here are 3 GitHub issues/tweets showing the pain. My tool provides unified typed data across 10 exchanges at \$0.001/call. Here's the schema."*

<Info>
**This is the bar for Execute-mode tools.** Show us the fragmented API landscape and the developers struggling with it.
</Info>

## 📋 Request for Builders (RFB)

We are no longer funding broad category wrappers like "crypto analytics" or "competitive intelligence."
We fund **feature-level products** with explicit demand, explicit evidence, and an explicit surface contract.
If you cannot explain why the buyer would rather pay through Context than own the workflow or integration themselves, the proposal is Tier B at best.

Every proposal should read like a product brief:

| You must specify | Query mode answer product | Execute mode primitive product |
|------------------|---------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Exact thing being unbundled** | The premium answer users currently pay for | The painful multi-source primitive developers currently normalize themselves |
| **Exact paying persona + why they pay through Context** | Trader, analyst, seller, researcher, or operator, plus why a managed Query answer beats manual search, dashboards, or a generic chatbot | Agent builder, workflow engineer, quant, or internal tools team, plus why they would pay Context instead of owning the integration directly |
| **5 must-win prompts** | Questions Context should answer materially better than a generic chatbot | Requests an agent can reliably compose into downstream logic |
| **Demand proof** | Specific evidence that people pay for or complain about this exact answer workflow, not just the category | Specific evidence that developers struggle with this exact integration or normalization pain, not just the category |
| **Expected evidence / schema** | Facts, source refs, freshness, confidence, artifact links, ideal view | Stable typed fields, pagination/filtering rules, error semantics |
| **Ideal output surface** | `Query` with `answer_with_evidence`, and optionally `evidence_only` for external agents | `Execute` or `both`, with normalized typed outputs |
| **Why Context wins vs DIY / free substitutes** | Dashboards, screenshots, docs, manual search, and generic chatbots are too slow or not machine-readable for the paid answer contract | Direct APIs, vendor SDKs, and in-house scripts have inconsistent schemas, auth models, missing normalization, or too much maintenance burden |

<Info>
**The north star:** submit a premium feature, not a generic category.

Good: "Exchange-flow intelligence with venue heatmaps, baseline comparison, and catalyst refs."

Weak: "Crypto analytics."

Good: "Cross-exchange order book normalization with one schema and stable pagination."

Weak: "Exchange API wrapper."
</Info>

## 🧠 Request for Builders: Query Mode Intelligence

In Query mode, **Context is the librarian**. Your tool should help Context produce a premium answer contract for both:

- first-party chat via `answer_with_evidence`
- external agents via `evidence_only`

That means your value is not just "the data exists." Your value is that the answer comes back with the right evidence fields, source refs, freshness, confidence, and a sensible render shape.

### Brief 1: Exchange-Flow Intelligence

*Replaces: Coinglass dashboards + manual spreadsheets + ad hoc news search*

- **Target user:** discretionary trader, research desk, exchange analyst
- **Why they pay:** they want a venue-level flow answer they can act on immediately, not screenshots and tab-hopping
- **Why free substitutes fail:** dashboards rarely produce a normalized ranking, baseline comparison, and explanation package in one machine-readable artifact
- **Must-win prompts:**
  - `Is BTC flowing into or out of exchanges on Coinglass? Search for news explaining the flow.`
  - `Which exchanges are seeing the largest BTC inflows and outflows over the last 24 hours? Give me a heatmap-style ranking with magnitude and share of total flow.`
  - `Where is stablecoin liquidity rotating today? Rank exchanges by USDT and USDC net inflow, then compare the move with the prior 7-day baseline.`
  - `Show me whether ETH exchange flows confirm or contradict current derivatives positioning. Compare net spot flows, open interest direction, and liquidations, and call out any venue-specific divergence.`
  - `Did BTC exchange flows flip from accumulation to distribution in the last 48 hours? Summarize the regime change, the leading venues, and the evidence behind the shift.`
- **Expected evidence fields:** `asset`, `assets`, `timeWindow`, `asOf`, `aggregateFlow`, `venueBreakdown`, `sourceRefs`, `confidence`, `assumptions`, `knownUnknowns`, plus baseline/catalyst/derivatives extensions when the prompt asks for them
- **Ideal output surface:** `Query`; preferred response shapes are `answer_with_evidence` for chat and `evidence_only` for external agents; ideal views are `heatmap`, `timeseries`, `leaderboard`, or `table`
- **Tier target:** **S**

### Brief 2: Prediction-Market Liquidity Intelligence

*Replaces: Polymarket/Kalshi browsing plus manual orderbook inspection*

- **Target user:** trader, market maker, newsletter writer, sports/politics analyst
- **Why they pay:** they need spread, depth, slippage, and venue comparison fast enough to trade or publish on it
- **Why free substitutes fail:** raw market pages show pieces of the picture, but not a reusable liquidity verdict with comparable fields
- **Must-win prompts:**
  - `Analyze liquidity for the World Cup winner market.`
  - `Which active Kalshi markets can absorb a $5,000 position with less than 2 cents of slippage?`
  - `Compare liquidity and pricing efficiency for the same event on Polymarket and Kalshi.`
  - `What is the cheapest way to exit a large YES position in this market right now?`
  - `Which active markets have the widest spread relative to their recent volume?`
- **Expected evidence fields:** `eventId`, `marketId`, `venue`, `side`, `bestBid`, `bestAsk`, `spreadBps`, `depth`, `slippage`, `recentVolume`, `observedAt`, `sourceRefs`
- **Ideal output surface:** `Query` or `both`; ideal views are `table`, `leaderboard`, and `heatmap`
- **Tier target:** **S**

### Brief 3: Filing and Earnings Delta Intelligence

*Replaces: Bloomberg / FactSet / Capital IQ workflows built on top of raw filings and transcripts*

- **Target user:** equity analyst, PM, founder, investor-relations advisor
- **Why they pay:** they want structured risk changes and management deltas without reading long PDFs or transcripts manually
- **Why free substitutes fail:** EDGAR is free, but extraction, comparison, and ranking are the premium layer
- **Must-win prompts:**
  - `What changed in this company's risk factors versus the previous 10-K?`
  - `Summarize the biggest guidance changes in the last two earnings calls.`
  - `Which recent filings show the sharpest increase in supply-chain risk language?`
  - `Compare management tone and guidance changes across three semiconductor companies this quarter.`
  - `What are the three most important accounting or legal red flags in this filing?`
- **Expected evidence fields:** `company`, `filingType`, `period`, `section`, `excerpt`, `changeType`, `changeMagnitude`, `sourceRefs`, `publishedAt`, `confidence`
- **Ideal output surface:** `Query`; ideal views are `table`, `diff`, and `leaderboard`
- **Tier target:** **S**

### Brief 4: App Download and Keyword-Share Intelligence

*Replaces: SensorTower / Data.ai subscription slices where the user only needs one premium answer*

- **Target user:** growth marketer, app founder, investor, product lead
- **Why they pay:** they need one concrete market-intel answer before a pitch, launch, or budget decision
- **Why free substitutes fail:** public app-store pages do not expose reliable download estimates, keyword-share trends, or competitor deltas
- **Must-win prompts:**
  - `Estimate downloads and revenue for this bundle ID over the last 30 days.`
  - `Which competitors gained the most keyword share this week in this category?`
  - `Compare download momentum for these three apps after the latest product launch.`
  - `What is the fastest-growing finance app in Brazil this month and what evidence supports that call?`
  - `Which ASO keywords matter most for this app and how did that change from last month?`
- **Expected evidence fields:** `bundleId`, `country`, `timeWindow`, `downloadEstimate`, `revenueEstimate`, `keywordShare`, `rankChange`, `sourceRefs`, `freshness`, `confidence`
- **Ideal output surface:** `Query`; ideal views are `leaderboard`, `timeseries`, and `table`
- **Tier target:** **S**

<Note>
**What Query proposals should look like:** a premium answer contract with must-win prompts, evidence fields, freshness targets, and a clear reason a user would pay for one answer instead of assembling it manually.
</Note>

## 🔌 Request for Builders: Execute Mode Infrastructure

In Execute mode, **the developer's agent is the librarian**. The product is a normalized primitive contract, not a prose answer.

You are selling:

- consistent field names
- predictable pagination/filtering
- stable method semantics
- explicit execute pricing
- fewer hours spent normalizing fragmented APIs

### Brief 1: Cross-Exchange Order Book and Price Normalizer

*Replaces: integrating Binance + Coinbase + Kraken + OKX + Bybit separately*

- **Target user:** quant, execution bot builder, analytics engineer
- **Why they pay:** they want one schema and one pagination/filter contract instead of maintaining exchange-specific adapters
- **Must-win requests:**
  - `get_prices` across multiple exchanges with one `{ exchange, pair, price, volume, timestamp }` shape
  - `get_orderbook` with the same depth schema on every venue
  - `get_funding_rates` with normalized exchange and contract identifiers
  - `get_liquidations` with consistent timestamps and side labels
  - `get_market_snapshot` that combines top-of-book, funding, and open interest into one typed response
- **Expected schema fields:** `exchange`, `symbol`, `timestamp`, `bids`, `asks`, `price`, `volume`, `fundingRate`, `openInterest`, `latencyNote`
- **Ideal output surface:** `Execute` or `both`
- **Tier target:** **S**

### Brief 2: Multi-Source Company Identity and Enrichment Normalizer

*Replaces: integrating Crunchbase + PitchBook + LinkedIn + BuiltWith + SimilarWeb separately*

- **Target user:** sales ops, internal tooling team, growth engineer
- **Why they pay:** entity matching and schema cleanup are the expensive part, not fetching one record
- **Must-win requests:**
  - resolve one company from domain, name, and LinkedIn slug into a stable company ID
  - fetch funding, employee, tech-stack, and traffic fields in one typed payload
  - paginate comparable companies with the same schema regardless of source
  - fetch hiring signals with normalized role/location fields
  - return provenance per field so downstream systems can decide what to trust
- **Expected schema fields:** `companyId`, `canonicalName`, `domain`, `sourceCoverage`, `employeeCount`, `funding`, `techStack`, `trafficEstimate`, `hiringSignals`, `provenance`
- **Ideal output surface:** `Execute` or `both`
- **Tier target:** **S**

### Brief 3: Multi-Retailer Pricing and Review Feed

*Replaces: integrating Amazon + Walmart + Target + Shopify endpoints separately*

- **Target user:** pricing bot builder, catalog ops team, e-commerce analyst
- **Why they pay:** they need reliable downstream joins and comparisons, not one-off scraping results
- **Must-win requests:**
  - compare one product across multiple retailers with one price schema
  - fetch historical prices with aligned timestamps and currency handling
  - normalize review counts, star ratings, and review text summaries
  - paginate search results across retailers with stable product identifiers
  - return structured availability and seller metadata
- **Expected schema fields:** `retailer`, `productId`, `canonicalSku`, `price`, `currency`, `availability`, `seller`, `reviewSummary`, `timestamp`
- **Ideal output surface:** `Execute` or `both`
- **Tier target:** **A**

<Note>
**Execute-mode tools can also serve Query mode.** If you set `surface: "both"` and `queryEligible: true` in your `_meta`, Context can use your normalized data as a source for premium Query answers too. See [Choosing a Mode](/guides/tool-metadata#choosing-a-mode).
</Note>

    - **Exact premium feature or normalized primitive** you are shipping
    - **Target paying user** and why they would pay for this instead of using free substitutes
    - **5 must-win prompts** that prove the product contract
    - **Expected evidence fields or normalized schema fields**
    - **Ideal output surface**: `Query`, `Execute`, or `both`
    - **Freshness / latency target** and any ambiguity behavior expectations
    - **What expensive service or fragmented integration this replaces**
    - Which tier you're targeting
    - Technical approach (1-2 paragraphs)
    - Your background (GitHub, portfolio)
    
    👉 [**Submit Proposal**](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSfM702YEtiCK5o22bA6hbM7cke7psDbfx4t9-Oj7OsnPjDhtA/viewform)

  </Step>
  <Step title="Request Review">
    <Warning>

    - **5 must-win prompts** that exercise the premium feature or normalized primitive you are claiming
    - **Expected evidence fields / expected schema outputs** for each prompt
    - **Expected response shape** for Query (`answer_with_evidence` and optionally `evidence_only`) or the exact Execute methods that should pass
    - Your **wallet address** (for payment verification)
    
    <Tip>
    **Make sure your listing description is complete.** If you used the [MCP Server Analysis Prompt](https://github.com/ctxprotocol/sdk/blob/main/docs/mcp-server-analysis-prompt.md) in the Contribute step, your description should already include Features, Try Asking questions, and Agent Tips. Reviews that find a poor or generic description will be sent back for improvement.
    </Tip>
    
    We'll test your tool and verify it meets our quality standards before activating grant payments.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Get Paid">

    - Update your listing response price from \$0 to your desired response fee

    - Agents discover and use your tool across Query + Execute
    - You earn 90% of eligible fees in USDC
    - Grant payment follows the schedule below
  </Step>
