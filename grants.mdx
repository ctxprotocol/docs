<Warning>
**🚫 What We Don't Fund**

We reject proposals that:

- **Wrap a single public API without adding value**: Calling CoinGecko and returning the JSON isn't a product. For Query mode, add analysis, scoring, or synthesis. For Execute mode, normalize across multiple sources into a consistent typed schema — that's the value. If the likely buyer would rather own the integration than pay through Context, it is Tier B at best unless you add strong normalization, provenance, partial-failure handling, caching, rate-limit protection, or multi-source abstraction.

As users shift to AI chatbots, these subscriptions are in structural decline. We don't fund cheaper versions of dying products. Ask: "Is the subscription I'm unbundling itself being disrupted by AI?" If yes, don't apply.
- **Compete with dominant free alternatives**: If Semantic Scholar, Etherscan, or Google News already does it for free, your tool needs a 10x differentiated angle.
- **Lack evidence of demand**: "This would be useful" isn't enough. Show us the expensive subscription people are paying for (Query tools) or the fragmented APIs developers are struggling to normalize (Execute tools). The evidence must support the exact feature or primitive you are pitching, not just the broad vertical.

**The bar:** Can you find real Twitter threads, forum posts, or Reddit threads of people complaining or evangelising about paying for this specific answer product — or developers complaining about the exact integration and normalization pain your Execute primitive solves? If not, don't apply.
</Warning>

## 🔍 How to Find Unbundling Opportunities

Before pitching, do this research. Here's a real example of how to identify a fundable tool:

### Example: Finding a SensorTower Unbundling Opportunity

**Step 1: Search for frustrated paying users**

Go to [Grok](https://grok.com) and search Twitter:
- `"SensorTower" "pay" OR "subscription" OR "expensive"`
- `"app analytics" "not worth" OR "canceled" OR "only need"`

**Step 2: Find the specific pain point**

You might find tweets like:
> *"I pay \$400/month for SensorTower just to check competitor download estimates before investor calls. Everything else in the dashboard I never touch."*

> *"SensorTower is worth it for the App Store keyword rankings alone. Rest is noise."*

**Step 3: Isolate the \$0.10 response**

From this research, you'd identify: **"App download estimates by bundle ID"** is the core value. Build a tool that delivers just that. The specific data point people are paying \$5K/year to access occasionally.

**Step 4: Pitch with evidence**

Your proposal should say: *"SensorTower charges \$5K/year. Users on Twitter report paying primarily for download estimates. Here's my tool that delivers that specific data point for \$0.10/response. Here are 3 tweets proving demand."*

<Info>
**This is the bar for Query-mode tools.** Every idea in the curated intelligence section has been evaluated against this standard.
</Info>

### Example: Finding an Execute-Mode Normalization Opportunity

**Step 1: Search for frustrated developers**

Go to [Grok](https://grok.com) and search Twitter, GitHub issues, or Reddit:
- `"different schema" "exchange" OR "API" site:github.com`
- `"normalize" "crypto" "API" "pain" OR "nightmare" OR "inconsistent"`

**Step 2: Find the integration pain**

You might find posts like:
> *"Spent 3 days just getting Binance, Coinbase, and Kraken order book data into the same format. Every exchange returns depth differently."*

> *"Why does every SEC filing parser return a completely different JSON structure? I just want balance sheet data in a consistent schema."*

**Step 3: Isolate the \$0.001 method**

From this research, you'd identify: **"normalized order book data across exchanges"** is the value. Build a tool where `get_orderbook("BTC/USDT", "binance")` and `get_orderbook("BTC/USDT", "kraken")` return the exact same typed schema. The developer pays fractions of a cent per call instead of building and maintaining the normalization themselves.

**Step 4: Pitch with evidence**

Your proposal should say: *"Developers integrating multiple exchange APIs spend days normalizing schemas. Here are 3 GitHub issues/tweets showing the pain. My tool provides unified typed data across 10 exchanges at \$0.001/call. Here's the schema."*

<Info>
**This is the bar for Execute-mode tools.** Show us the fragmented API landscape and the developers struggling with it.
</Info>

## 📋 Request for Builders (RFB)

We no longer fund broad category wrappers like "crypto analytics," "competitive intelligence," or "financial research."

We fund **feature-level products** with:

- a clear buyer
- a painful workflow
- an expensive current substitute
- a narrow, testable surface contract
- an obvious reason to exist on Context instead of as a generic dashboard or API wrapper

The best RFBs are not organized by protocol mode first. They are organized by **buyer pain and workflow**.

Mode still matters, but it should usually be a property of the product:

- `Query`: the buyer wants a premium answer with evidence
- `Execute`: the buyer wants a narrow typed primitive
- `Both`: the same underlying data can support both

<Info>
**The north star:** submit a premium feature, not a generic category.

Good: `BTC/ETH exchange-flow regime intelligence`

Weak: `Crypto analytics`

Good: `Cross-venue perp funding / OI / liquidation normalizer`

Weak: `Exchange API wrapper`
</Info>

### What Every Strong RFB Includes

Every serious proposal should read like a product brief and answer all of the following:

- **Buyer:** who pays for this answer or primitive?
- **Pain:** what annoying or expensive workflow are they replacing?
- **Current substitute:** which paid product, dashboard, terminal workflow, or internal script are you unbundling?
- **Mode fit:** why should this be `Query`, `Execute`, or `both`?
- **Must-win prompts or methods:** what are the 3-5 tests that prove the product works?
- **Evidence or schema contract:** what fields, provenance, freshness, and output shape should review expect?
- **Scope boundary:** what are you *not* trying to build in v1?
- **Why Context wins:** why would the buyer rather pay through Context than do this manually or own the integration themselves?

<Warning>
**What weak applications still get wrong**

- broad claims like "Bloomberg for AI" or "CoinGlass for agents"
- no real buyer, just "this data is interesting"
- no wedge versus free search, dashboards, or manual workflows
- too many sources and too much normalization for a solo builder
- products that are really alerting feeds or dashboard SaaS products
- raw data wrappers with no evidence layer, no schema discipline, and no differentiation
</Warning>

## 📌 RFBs We Actually Want

### Crypto Market Structure

#### 1. BTC / ETH Exchange-Flow Regime Intelligence

- **Mode:** `Query`
- **Buyer:** discretionary trader, research desk, exchange analyst
- **Replaces:** CoinGlass spot inflow/outflow dashboards, spreadsheets, and manual venue comparison
- **Why this belongs on Context:** the buyer wants one answer that says where flows are moving, whether the move is unusual versus baseline, and whether it looks like accumulation, distribution, or liquidity rotation
- **What to deliver:** a premium answer contract with venue rankings, baseline comparison, and evidence-rich explanation
- **Strong submissions:** start with BTC and ETH, a limited venue set, explicit evidence fields, and clear freshness targets
- **Weak submissions:** "crypto analytics platform," generic dashboards, or trying to cover every exchange and every asset at once
- **Must-win prompts:**
- `Which exchanges are seeing the largest BTC inflows and outflows over the last 24 hours, and is the move unusual versus the prior 7-day baseline?`
- `Did ETH exchange flows flip from accumulation to distribution in the last 48 hours?`
- `Where is stablecoin liquidity rotating today across major venues?`
- **Why we'd fund this:** clear buyer, obvious willingness to pay, strong Query fit

#### 2. Liquidation Heatmap and Squeeze-Risk Intelligence

- **Mode:** `Query`
- **Buyer:** perp trader, intraday trader, market commentator
- **Replaces:** Hyblock or CoinGlass liquidation maps plus manual interpretation
- **Why this belongs on Context:** traders do not just want the picture; they want the answer about where forced positioning is clustered and what price zones matter right now
- **What to deliver:** a queryable answer about liquidation clusters, nearby pressure zones, and whether the setup favors squeeze risk
- **Strong submissions:** transparent methodology, tight asset scope, venue and timestamp evidence
- **Weak submissions:** black-box "AI liquidation levels" with no provenance or explainability
- **Must-win prompts:**
- `Where are the largest nearby BTC liquidation clusters right now?`
- `Is the current ETH setup more vulnerable to a long squeeze or short squeeze?`
- `Which venue shows the most concentrated liquidation pressure in SOL today?`
- **Why we'd fund this:** one of the clearest paid answer products in crypto

#### 3. Funding / Open-Interest Divergence Screener

- **Mode:** `Query` or `both`
- **Buyer:** perp trader, strategist, newsletter writer
- **Replaces:** CoinGlass or Hyblock funding and OI tabs across multiple exchanges
- **Why this belongs on Context:** the buyer wants a ranked answer about crowded positioning, not ten exchange tabs
- **What to deliver:** ranked divergence answers, and optionally normalized methods for funding, OI, and liquidation data
- **Strong submissions:** narrow venue scope, explicit ranking logic, stable field names
- **Weak submissions:** generic exchange wrapper with no opinionated product contract
- **Must-win prompts:**
- `Which assets have the strongest funding / OI divergence right now?`
- `Show me assets where open interest is rising while funding is rolling over.`
- `Which venues are most crowded long in BTC perps today?`
- **Why we'd fund this:** strong Query fit and also a good `Execute` primitive if the schema is disciplined

#### 4. ETF Flows + Holdings + Basis Dislocation Intelligence

- **Mode:** `both`
- **Buyer:** crypto PM, allocator, macro desk
- **Replaces:** Bloomberg terminal checks, issuer pages, ETF dashboards, and manual basis comparisons
- **Why this belongs on Context:** the buyer wants one answer tying flows, holdings changes, and market structure together, or one typed method they can call in a workflow
- **What to deliver:** ETF flow and holdings answers with context, and optionally a small set of normalized methods
- **Strong submissions:** durable sources, explicit freshness, narrow ETF coverage to start
- **Weak submissions:** daily ETF tables with no interpretation or basis context
- **Must-win prompts or methods:**
- `Which spot BTC ETFs saw the largest net inflows this week, and how does that compare to price and basis?`
- `Did ETF demand confirm or contradict today's BTC move?`
- `get_etf_flows`, `get_etf_holdings`, or similarly narrow typed methods
- **Why we'd fund this:** durable demand, durable sources, obvious buyer

### On-Chain Positioning

#### 5. Whale Accumulation and Exchange-Pressure Intelligence

- **Mode:** `Query`
- **Buyer:** crypto macro trader, swing trader, on-chain researcher
- **Replaces:** Glassnode whale dashboards plus manual interpretation of exchange balances and flows
- **Why this belongs on Context:** the job is not "show me whale data"; the job is "tell me whether large holders are accumulating and whether supply pressure is building on exchanges"
- **What to deliver:** an answer that combines large-holder behavior with exchange supply pressure and clear provenance
- **Strong submissions:** explicit entity definitions, limited asset scope, evidence fields for balances, transfers, and time windows
- **Weak submissions:** generic whale-alert feeds or unlabeled wallet theater
- **Must-win prompts:**
- `Are BTC whales accumulating or distributing right now?`
- `Is exchange supply pressure rising or falling for ETH this week?`
- `Do whale transfers support the current market move or contradict it?`
- **Why we'd fund this:** strong buyer pain, strong premium-answer fit

#### 6. LTH / STH Supply Regime-Shift Intelligence

- **Mode:** `Query`
- **Buyer:** BTC macro trader, allocator, content creator serving serious investors
- **Replaces:** Glassnode cohort dashboards and manual cycle interpretation
- **Why this belongs on Context:** users pay to understand whether long-term holders are distributing, whether short-term holders are trapped, and whether the market regime is changing
- **What to deliver:** a regime answer contract grounded in holder-cohort metrics, not a raw metric dump
- **Strong submissions:** one or two assets, clearly named regime states, cited evidence
- **Weak submissions:** academic on-chain commentary with no decision usefulness
- **Must-win prompts:**
- `Are long-term holders distributing into this BTC rally?`
- `Has short-term holder pain reached washout territory?`
- `Did the market regime shift in the last 30 days based on holder behavior?`
- **Why we'd fund this:** repeatable buyer value and strong differentiation from generic chatbots

#### 7. MVRV / NUPL / SOPR Market-Regime Composite

- **Mode:** `Query`
- **Buyer:** crypto macro trader, allocator, serious retail analyst
- **Replaces:** Glassnode cycle dashboards interpreted manually
- **Why this belongs on Context:** this is useful only when turned into a verdict on regime, froth, capitulation, or distribution risk
- **What to deliver:** a composite regime answer that explains how the underlying metrics line up and where confidence is low
- **Strong submissions:** a narrow, opinionated framework with transparent evidence
- **Weak submissions:** dumping 10 charts and calling it analysis
- **Must-win prompts:**
- `Is BTC overheated, neutral, or washed out based on cycle metrics right now?`
- `Do current SOPR, NUPL, and MVRV readings support continuation or distribution risk?`
- `How does today's regime compare to prior local tops or bottoms?`
- **Why we'd fund this:** buyers already pay for this workflow; the value is the synthesis, not the chart

### Public Markets and Document Workflows

#### 8. SEC Filing Delta Intelligence

- **Mode:** `Query`
- **Buyer:** equity analyst, PM, founder, investor-relations advisor
- **Replaces:** Bloomberg / FactSet workflows built on top of raw filings and transcripts
- **Why this belongs on Context:** EDGAR is free, but diffing, extraction, ranking, and synthesis are the paid layer
- **What to deliver:** structured deltas in risk factors, guidance language, legal exposure, or other decision-relevant sections
- **Strong submissions:** deterministic section extraction, diffable evidence, clean citations
- **Weak submissions:** generic filing summarizers with vague "AI insights"
- **Must-win prompts:**
- `What changed in this company's risk factors versus the previous 10-K?`
- `What are the three most important new legal or accounting red flags in this filing?`
- `Which recent semiconductor filings show the sharpest increase in supply-chain risk language?`
- **Why we'd fund this:** durable public source, obvious premium workflow, strong reviewability

#### 9. Earnings Estimate Revision Momentum

- **Mode:** `Query` or `both`
- **Buyer:** PM, analyst, event-driven investor
- **Replaces:** Bloomberg EE and revision workflows
- **Why this belongs on Context:** the buyer wants a compact answer about where revisions are moving and whether the move is broad-based, not a terminal screen
- **What to deliver:** a ranked revision answer product, and optionally a typed primitive for revision data
- **Strong submissions:** clear methodology, narrow sector or geography to start, evidence fields for estimate changes and time windows
- **Weak submissions:** broad equity screener with vague "analyst sentiment"
- **Must-win prompts or methods:**
- `Which companies in semis saw the strongest positive earnings revisions over the last 30 days?`
- `Where is estimate momentum collapsing ahead of earnings this quarter?`
- `get_estimate_revisions` or similarly narrow methods
- **Why we'd fund this:** direct buyer pain, high-value workflow, good Query and Execute fit

#### 10. Insider Cluster-Buy Intelligence

- **Mode:** `Query` or `both`
- **Buyer:** small-cap investor, event-driven analyst
- **Replaces:** Bloomberg insider screens plus manual Form 4 review
- **Why this belongs on Context:** the buyer does not want every insider trade; they want the ones that matter, ranked by conviction and context
- **What to deliver:** answers or methods that identify meaningful cluster buying, role importance, and surrounding company context
- **Strong submissions:** explicit thresholds, role-aware ranking, provenance to the original filing
- **Weak submissions:** noisy alert feeds, retail-pump framing, or undifferentiated insider scraping
- **Must-win prompts:**
- `Which U.S. small caps saw the strongest insider cluster buying this month?`
- `Is this insider buying meaningful or just routine compensation noise?`
- `Which recent insider-buy clusters coincide with improving fundamentals or estimate revisions?`
- **Why we'd fund this:** strong buyer, narrow scope, very reviewable

#### 11. Supply-Chain Shock Exposure Intelligence

- **Mode:** `Query`
- **Buyer:** PM, credit analyst, corporate strategy team
- **Replaces:** Bloomberg SPLC plus manual tariff, sanctions, bankruptcy, or supplier-risk research
- **Why this belongs on Context:** the buyer wants a fast answer about exposure to a real-world shock, not a giant supply-chain graph
- **What to deliver:** a narrow exposure answer product around a specific risk class such as tariffs, sanctions, concentration risk, or supplier distress
- **Strong submissions:** one shock type at a time, cited relationships, exposure scoring with assumptions clearly stated
- **Weak submissions:** generic "supply-chain intelligence platform"
- **Must-win prompts:**
- `Which semiconductor names are most exposed to a Taiwan shock through critical suppliers?`
- `Which U.S. industrials face the highest tariff exposure under this scenario?`
- `Which companies are most exposed to a distressed supplier in this chain?`
- **Why we'd fund this:** commercially sharp and much more valuable than generic news summarization

|--------------|-------------------|
| `Crypto analytics platform` | too broad, too saturated, and no clear review surface |
| `Bloomberg for agents` | impossible scope, unclear buyer contract, guaranteed hand-waving |
| generic public API wrapper | easy to replace, weak wedge, usually Tier B at best |
| pure monitoring feed / alerts product | belongs in a dashboard SaaS, not a paid Context answer |
| unscoped multi-source normalizer | too much maintenance for a solo builder and too much schema risk |
| social sentiment / narrative dashboard | saturated, noisy, weak differentiation, poor reviewability |

If your pitch sounds like "I will aggregate everything and let the AI figure it out," it is almost certainly too broad.

If your pitch sounds like "I am unbundling this one paid workflow from a product serious users already buy," you are probably on the right track.

## 📝 How to Apply

<Warning>
**One application at a time.** If you have an active grant project (Processing, Draft Created, Conversation, or any awarded tier), complete it before submitting a new application. Duplicate submissions will be automatically rejected.

**Use your GitHub email.** Submit your application using the same email address associated with your GitHub account (your public email or the email in your commit history). We verify GitHub ownership automatically — if your application email doesn't match your GitHub, we'll ask you to prove you own the account before we can review your proposal.
</Warning>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Your Account">
    Sign in at [ctxprotocol.com](https://ctxprotocol.com) to create your account. This generates your **embedded wallet** the address where you'll receive grants and revenue.
    
    <Tip>
    Copy your wallet address from the user nav menu in the bottom left of the main chat app. You'll need it for the application form.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit Proposal">
    Fill out our application form with:
    - **Your email** — use the same email that's on your GitHub (public profile email or commit email). We verify GitHub ownership automatically, and mismatched emails will delay your review.
    - **Your wallet address** (from Step 1 — required for payment)
    - Tool name and description
    - **Exact premium feature or normalized primitive** you are shipping
    - **Target paying user** and why they would pay for this instead of using free substitutes
    - **5 must-win prompts** that prove the product contract
    - **Expected evidence fields or normalized schema fields**
    - **Ideal output surface**: `Query`, `Execute`, or `both`
    - **Freshness / latency target** and any ambiguity behavior expectations
    - **What expensive service or fragmented integration this replaces**
    - Which tier you're targeting
    - Technical approach (1-2 paragraphs)
    - Your background (GitHub, portfolio)
    
    👉 [**Submit Proposal**](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSfM702YEtiCK5o22bA6hbM7cke7psDbfx4t9-Oj7OsnPjDhtA/viewform)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build & Deploy">
    Once approved, build your MCP server using our SDK:
    
    ```bash
    npm install @ctxprotocol/sdk @modelcontextprotocol/sdk
    ```
    
    **Constraint:** Tools must return data in under 60 seconds. Focus on instant retrieval and analysis.
    
    Follow the [Build Tools Guide](/guides/build-tools) for the complete pattern.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Register Your Tool">
    Go to [ctxprotocol.com/contribute](https://ctxprotocol.com/contribute) to register your tool:
    - Paste your MCP endpoint URL
    - Add a basic name and description (the optimization skill will generate and push the optimal description in the next step)
    - **Set your listing response price to \$0** for initial testing (you can change it after we review)
    - **Advanced (optional):** If you also want SDK developers to call your methods directly with per-call billing ([Execute mode](/guides/tool-metadata#pricing-&-execute-mode-eligibility)), set a default execute price — typically ~1/100 of your response price. Skip this if you're starting with Query mode only; you can enable it later.
    - Add stake to activate your tool
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optimize & Validate">
    Before requesting review, run the full optimization workflow:
    
    **Recommended:** Load the **[MCP Tool Optimization Skill](https://github.com/ctxprotocol/sdk/blob/main/docs/mcp-tool-optimization-skill.md)** into your AI coding agent (Cursor, Claude Code, etc.) with your API key and Tool ID. It researches your vertical, proves your tool beats free LLMs, fixes issues, generates an optimal description, and pushes it to your listing automatically.
    
    The skill produces a structured **optimization artifact** — a machine-readable proof of differentiation. **Tier S grants require this artifact** (see Quality Requirements below).
    
    Also test manually in the Chat app:
    - Enable **Developer Mode** in Settings → Developer Settings
    - In the right sidebar tool selector turn off **Auto mode** and select your tool
    - Send a message to query your tool — you should see a response
    - Check the **Developer Logs** preview card at the bottom of the response for errors
    - Click **Copy All** on the logs and paste into an AI assistant to diagnose issues
    
    See the full [Debugging Guide](/guides/troubleshooting#using-developer-mode-for-debugging) for detailed instructions.
    
    <Tip>
    **Lightweight alternative:** For a quick protocol-compliance check without full optimization, use the [Deep Validation System Prompt](https://github.com/ctxprotocol/sdk/blob/main/docs/mcp-contributor-deep-validation-system-prompt.md). Use the optimization skill for grant applications, especially Tier S.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Request Review">
    <Warning>
    Your tool **must be live on the marketplace** before requesting review. If you skip the "Contribute to Marketplace" and "Debug & Refine" steps above, your review will be returned and you'll be asked to complete those steps first.
    </Warning>
    
    Once your tool is submitted, tested, and working correctly on the marketplace, email **grants@ctxprotocol.com** with:
    - Your **tool name** and **endpoint URL**
    - Your **tool ID** from ctxprotocol.com (go to [Developer → Tools](https://ctxprotocol.com/developer/tools) and copy the ID shown on your tool card — this is how we identify your tool for testing)
    - Link to your **public GitHub repo** for the MCP server (for code review)
    - **5 must-win prompts** that exercise the premium feature or normalized primitive you are claiming
    - **Expected evidence fields / expected schema outputs** for each prompt
    - **Expected response shape** for Query (`answer_with_evidence` and optionally `evidence_only`) or the exact Execute methods that should pass
    - Your **wallet address** (for payment verification)
    
    <Tip>
    **Make sure your listing description is complete.** If you used the [MCP Server Analysis Prompt](https://github.com/ctxprotocol/sdk/blob/main/docs/mcp-server-analysis-prompt.md) in the Contribute step, your description should already include Features, Try Asking questions, and Agent Tips. Reviews that find a poor or generic description will be sent back for improvement.
    </Tip>
    
    We'll test your tool and verify it meets our quality standards before activating grant payments.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Get Paid">
    Once verified, you're live on the marketplace!
    - Update your listing response price from \$0 to your desired response fee
    - Optionally enable execute pricing for methods you want in Execute mode
    - Agents discover and use your tool across Query + Execute
    - You earn 90% of eligible fees in USDC
    - Grant payment follows the schedule below
  </Step>
</Steps>

## ❓ FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How long does approval take?">
    We review proposals within **48 hours**. Complex proposals may require a quick call.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I build multiple tools?">
    Yes, but one at a time. Complete your current grant project before applying for another. This ensures quality and focus.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if my tool idea isn't on the RFB list?">
    The RFB is a starting point, not a limit. If your idea is not listed, pitch it using the same contract: exact feature, target user, 5 must-win prompts, expected evidence fields or normalized schema, ideal output surface, and proof that free substitutes are insufficient.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I keep the revenue after the grant?">
    Yes! The grant is a one-time bootstrap. You keep **90% of all eligible usage fees** forever that's the whole point.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What chain is payment on?">
    All payments (grants and revenue) are in **USDC on Base**. Low fees, deferred settlement (settled automatically after successful execution).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I use existing open-source code?">
    Yes, as long as you comply with the license. Wrapping an existing API or open-source tool is fine just make it useful for agents.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What's the response time limit?">
    Tools must return data in **under 60 seconds**. This isn't the place for long-running simulations focus on instant data retrieval and analysis.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
